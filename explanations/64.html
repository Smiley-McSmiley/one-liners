This graph is relatively easy to wrap your head around.
Points can be thought of as a distance from the origin and an angle to the x-axis.
In the case of this graph, it is plotting every point (r,θ) for which θ < π sin r.
Looking closely, it's the positive part of a sine wave wrapped around so that it covers the entire graph.

Those of you who have used Desmos before may have noticed something strange, though.
Desmos cannot plot graphs of polar equations that are nonlinear in r.
So how was this plotted?

Depending on where you live, you may already be familiar with these substitutions.
The distance a point is from the origin (which is assigned as r here) is equivalent to √x²+y².
The angle of a point to the x-axis is usually taught as atan(y/x), but this runs into issues I struggle to explain.
The programmers will recognize this problem, and will know about atan2(). Desmos has this built into the arctangent function.
